What Is Femicide?

Femicide is the intentional killing of women and girls because of their gender.It includes various forms of violence against women such as intimate partner violence , honor killings and dowry related killings .

Learn More

Causes of Femicide.

Gender roles

Gender roles deeply rooted in Kenya societies perpetuate a patriarchal system that devalues women. Patriarchy creates a culture of power imbalance where men feel entitled to control and dominate women both within and outside the household.

Traditional practices

Traditional practices such as female genital mutilation, child marriages and wife inheritance further perpetuate the vulnerability and defeat of women. These practices condone violence against women and perpetuate harmful gender norms.

Economic disparities

Poverty and lack of education, healthcare and employment disproportionately affects women. Women when economically dependent on their partners or families, they are often trapped in abusive relationships without escape.

Society’s Normalization

Society’s normalization and acceptance of violence against women. Many cases of domestic violence go unreported due to fear, stigma or lack of trust in the justice system.

PREVENTION

1. Awareness Campaigns

Conducting awareness campaigns to educate the public about the prevalence, signs, causes, and consequences of femicide and homicide.
2. Advocate for Laws

Advocate for and support the implementation of laws that specifically address violence against women and ensure that the legal framework is comprehensive, enforceable, and provides adequate protection for victims.
3. Empowerment and Economic Independence

Efforts must be made to empower women economically. This can be achieved through vocational training, access to micro-financing, and opportunities for entrepreneurship. Women who are financially independent are better equipped to escape abusive relationships and have control over their lives.
4. Support Services and Safe Spaces

Accessible support services including helplines, shelters, and counseling centers are crucial for survivors of homicide and femicide. These safe spaces provide survivors with the support they need to rebuild their lives and break free from the cycle of abuse.
5. Addressing Domestic Violence

Addressing domestic violence through education, counseling, and support for victims can help prevent homicide and femicide related to domestic violence.
6. Strengthening Gun Control Laws

Guns are the most common weapon used in homicide. Strengthening gun control laws like implementing background checks and limiting access to guns for people with a history of violence and mental illness can reduce femicide and homicide.

Homicide

Homicide is the killing of one person by another with intent to cause death or serious injury by any means.

Causes of Homicide

  • Domestic violence. It occurs when a partner or family member uses physical, emotional or sexual abuse to control and dominate another person.
  • Gang violence. Gang often engages in drug trafficking, territorials disputes and revenge killings.
  • Mental illness. People with mental illness will be more likely to commit homicide.
  • Substance abuse. Particularly alcohol and drugs is often a factor in homicide.

Types of Homicide

  • Manslaughter: is a non premeditated killing of another person.e.g if you accidentally run over a cyclist while driving
  • Murder : involves the specific intent. It is unlawful killing of another human without justification.
  • Justifiable homicide : occurs when someone kills another person in self defense or defense of others.

Effects of Femicide and Homicide

  • Loss of human life: This is the most immediate and profound consequence of Femicide and homicide. Each victim is a unique individual with hopes, dreams, and contributions to society, and their untimely death leaves behind grieving families and communities.

  • Trauma and psychological impact: Survivors of these forms of violence, as well as those who witnessed or are affected by these acts, often experience profound trauma and psychological distress. The trauma can manifest in various forms including depression, anxiety, and feelings of guilt or survivor's guilt.

  • Disruption of families and communities: Femicide and homicides tear apart families and communities, leaving behind fractured financial hardship and social disintegration. The losses of loved ones because of these acts have long-lasting effects on family dynamics, particularly for children who may lose a parent or a caregiver.

  • Undermining human rights and dignity: Femicide and homicides represent violations of human rights and dignity, denying individuals their fundamental right to life, safety, and security.

  • Economic and social cost: The economic and social costs of these forms of violence are substantial, encompassing expenses related to healthcare, law enforcement, legal proceedings, and victim-related services. The loss of productive members of society, particularly women and young people, can have long-term implications for economic development and social cohesion.

  • Cycle of violence and retaliations: In some cases, Femicide and homicide perpetuates cycles of violence, fueling further acts of aggression and revenge which lead to increased harm and suffering for individuals and communities caught in its grip. Femicide and homicide manifest gender-based violence epidemics perpetuating violence against women and girls. These acts reinforce power imbalances and contribute to a culture of fear for women and marginalized gender identities.
